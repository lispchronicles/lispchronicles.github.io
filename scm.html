Scheme is a masterpiece of minimalism in programming language design. Being a simple language, it never ceases to amaze me with its expressive power. Its most widely implemented standard is only 50 pages long and can be read over a weekend. How does such a compactly defined language stack up against large, "industry standard" languages? What is the secret sauce of Scheme's minimal but effective design? These are the questions we will try to answer in this post. The insights gained may be applicable to genreal system design problems.
